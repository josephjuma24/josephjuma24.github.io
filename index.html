Voice Assistant & ASR for African Markets

Transsion (Caricare) · 2023–2025
Problem

Existing voice assistants and ASR systems were trained predominantly on Western English and did not account for the phonological diversity, code-switching, and ambient noise environments common to African mobile users. Adoption was low and accuracy was poor.

Approach
  • Conducted in-depth user interviews and contextual inquiry in urban and peri-urban Kenyan markets.
  • Designed multi-modal data collection pipelines to gather African-accented speech data.
  • Ran usability tests to identify failure modes in ASR and voice command interpretation.
  • Collaborated with engineering teams to feed research insights into model training and feature design.
Results

Contributed to a 30% improvement in voice command accuracy and measurably higher user adoption rates across African markets. Research outputs included user personas, journey maps, and annotated failure-case datasets.

AI Translation Tool for African Languages

Transsion (Caricare) · 2023–2025
Problem

Existing mobile translation systems performed poorly on African languages due to low training data availability, tonal language complexity, and lack of culturally contextual translation. This limited inclusivity for non-English-speaking users across the continent.

Approach
  • Evaluated existing AI-driven translation models for accuracy in African language contexts.
  • Conducted user testing with native speakers of Swahili, Hausa, Amharic, and other languages.
  • Identified cultural nuances and idiomatic expressions not captured by baseline models.
  • Provided structured recommendations to engineering teams for model fine-tuning.
Results

Translation quality improved for African language pairs, with research directly influencing model updates. Enabled more accurate and culturally relevant multilingual communication on mobile devices across African markets.

Multilingual NLP LLMs African Languages Evaluation Frameworks Qualitative Research
